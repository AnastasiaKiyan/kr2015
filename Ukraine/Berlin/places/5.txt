5.Kolonna Victory
Victory Column was built to commemorate the military exploits of the Prussian army against Denmark (1864), Austria (1866) and France (1871). First, its construction was planned to commemorate the victory in the War of Shlezving against Denmark in 1864, but by the time the monument was opened in 1874, new victory. Measure the height of a column of 69 meters and is located in the center of the Tiergarten (original location was the Royal Square, and in 1938 moved here) in the center of the ring road, which connects five major routes in Berlin. On the column is a golden statue of 8.3 meters in height and weighing 35 tons, which was added later, and represents the goddess Victoria.
